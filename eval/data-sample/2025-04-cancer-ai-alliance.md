# Ai2 and Google Cloud commit $20M to advance AI-powered research for the Cancer AI Alliance

**Date:** April 2025  
**Source:** [Allen Institute for AI](https://allenai.org/blog/ai2-google-next)

## Summary

The Allen Institute for AI (Ai2) and Google Cloud each committed $10 million to the Cancer AI Alliance (CAIA), totaling a $20 million investment aimed at accelerating cancer research through artificial intelligence.

## About Cancer AI Alliance

CAIA is a consortium led by the Fred Hutch Cancer Center, uniting leading cancer research institutions and technology companies:

### Founding Members (October 2024)
- Fred Hutch Cancer Center
- Dana-Farber Cancer Institute
- Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center
- Johns Hopkins

### Initial Backing
More than $40 million in initial support from:
- AWS
- Microsoft
- NVIDIA
- Deloitte
- Slalom

## Contribution Details

### Google Cloud
Providing advanced and secure technology infrastructure

### Ai2
Leading the training and development of AI models specifically for cancer research

## Technical Approach

- Novel AI infrastructure supporting training and deployment across institutions
- Maintains data security and privacy standards for patients
- Scalable, multi-cloud platform using federated learning
- Enables secure, multi-site research

## Long-Term Goal

The alliance aims to grow the initiative to $1 billion in resources, with the goal of revolutionizing cancer research through cutting-edge AI technology.
